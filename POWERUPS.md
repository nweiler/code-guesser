  Proposing Growth Powerups: Proposing "powerup" ideas to increase engagement and tastefully promote the user's work.


✦ That's a great idea! Sites like this are perfect "portfolio pieces" that can drive traffic to your other work. Here are some tasteful "powerups" to increase engagement and visibility:

  1. The "Personal Brand" Badge
  Instead of just a footer link, we could add a subtle "Made by @nweiler" badge in the top corner.
   * Engagement: Clicking it takes users to your GitHub profile or a "Featured Projects" page.
   * Vibe: Very standard for indie dev projects; feels personal and high-quality.

  2. Social Sharing (The "Brag" Factor)
  After a round or a streak, we can add a "Share on X/Twitter" button that pre-fills a fun message:
   * "I just guessed 8/10 repos correctly on CodeGuesser! 🎯 Can you beat my score? https://www.codeguesser.xyz"
   * Growth: This is the #1 way viral tools grow. It turns every player into a mini-marketer.

  3. "Suggest a Repository" (GitHub Traffic Driver)
  Add a button that says "Don't see your favorite repo? Suggest it here!"
   * Integration: This link goes directly to the "Issues" or "Discussions" page of your GitHub repo.
   * Vibe: It makes the project feel community-driven and drives literal traffic/stars to your repository.

  4. Streak Milestones & "Easter Eggs"
  If someone gets 5 or 10 correct in a row, we could trigger a special "Celebration" animation (confetti) with a message like:
   * "You're a pro! Check out some of my other open-source tools on GitHub."

  5. Global "Total Guesses" Counter
  A small ticker at the bottom: "12,432 repos guessed correctly by the community."
   * Vibe: It gives the site a "live" feel and makes new users feel like they're part of something active.
